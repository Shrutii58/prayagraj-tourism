Location: Uttar Pradesh, India

Prayagraj, historically known as Allahabad, is one of the most iconic cities in India. Nestled at the confluence of the holy rivers Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati, the city has been a focal point of Indian culture, spirituality, and history for thousands of years.

The city is an amalgamation of ancient heritage and modern growth. It has been a center of learning, spirituality, and administration since ancient times. Prayagraj is famous not only for its religious significance but also for its architectural marvels, vibrant culture, and bustling marketplaces.

The city‚Äôs tagline ‚ÄúCity of Confluence‚Äù is not just spiritual but also symbolic of its cultural diversity. From pilgrims to historians, food lovers to festival enthusiasts, Prayagraj welcomes all to explore its timeless treasures.

History & Significance

Prayagraj has a rich tapestry of history that stretches back to ancient times. The city is mentioned in several epic texts like the Mahabharata and has been an important center during the Maurya, Gupta, and Mughal periods.

The Triveni Sangam, the meeting point of the three rivers, has been a site of spiritual pilgrimage for centuries. Millions of devotees come to take a holy dip in the sacred waters, believing it cleanses their soul and grants blessings.

One of the city‚Äôs most iconic monuments is the Allahabad Fort, constructed by Emperor Akbar in 1583. This massive fort represents the grandeur of Mughal architecture ans stands as a testament to the city's glorious past.

Prayagraj is also the venue of the world-renowned Kumbh Mela, held every 12 years, which attracts millions of pilgrims and visitors from across the world .
